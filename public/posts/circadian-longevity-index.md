# Your Circadian Longevity Index

Your biological age is not determined by years, but by how your internal systems function.

## Understanding Biological Age

Through chronobiological modeling, we evaluate the strength, regularity, and phase of your circadian rhythms — powerful predictors of long-term healthspan.

Our system estimates your **biological age of circadian function**, also known as CosinorAge, which serves as a sensitive marker of healthspan and resilience.

## What Makes Circadian Age Different

Unlike chronological age, which simply counts years, circadian biological age reflects the actual functional state of your internal timing systems. This includes:

- **Sleep-wake cycles**: The synchronization of your rest and activity patterns
- **Metabolic rhythms**: How your body processes energy throughout the day
- **Activity patterns**: The regularity of your daily movements
- **Autonomic balance**: The coordination of your nervous system's regulation

## What a Lower Index Means

A lower index corresponds to:

- **Improved resilience**: Better ability to recover from stress and challenges
- **Metabolic stability**: More consistent energy regulation and blood sugar control
- **Cardiovascular efficiency**: Optimized heart rate variability and blood pressure patterns
- **Mood regulation**: Better emotional stability and cognitive function

## The Science Behind It

Chronobiological modeling uses advanced mathematical analysis to quantify the synchrony of your internal clocks. This approach is grounded in decades of research showing that circadian rhythm strength and regularity are powerful predictors of:

- Longevity and healthspan
- Disease risk
- Cognitive decline
- Metabolic health

## Example

A typical user might see:

**Chronological Age: 36 | Circadian Biological Age: 31**

This indicates that their internal systems are functioning at a level typically associated with someone 5 years younger, suggesting excellent circadian health and potential for extended healthspan.

## Why It Matters

By understanding your circadian biological age, you gain insight into how your lifestyle, environment, and habits are affecting your fundamental health systems. This knowledge enables targeted interventions to optimize your internal rhythms and support long-term vitality.
